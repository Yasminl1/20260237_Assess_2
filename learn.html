What Does Siva Mean?

In Samoan, "Siva" means dance.

Short summary about female siva

The siva is a traditional dance performed with slow, graceful, and flowing movements. Usually performed by a young woman, the dance incorporates storytelling through elegant gestures and body movements. Samoan siva is often performed at churches, family events, and cultural festivals such as Polyfest and Pasifika. Pacific Dance NZ also offers dance classes for young people, helping keep the tradition alive in New Zealand.

Taualuga

The word ‘Taualuga’ in Samoan dance is performed during the final and most important part of a celebration. Historically, it was danced by a young woman chosen to represent her family or village, often a chief’s daughter, but today it is commonly performed by the guest of honour, the dancer wears an ‘ie toga (fine mat) and a tuiga, a ceremonial headdress made from nautilus shell, red feathers, and ancestral hair, symbolising honour, respect, family heritage, and connection to ancestors. Through slow, graceful movements and hand gestures, the Taualuga expresses pride, respect, and cultural identity.

Siva Afi

The ‘Siva Afi’ is a fire-knife dance that showcases bravery, agility, and precision, and has strong connections to Samoa’s warrior traditions. The weapon used is called the ‘nifo oti’, and the dance involves twirling a large knife with burning flames at both ends. The performance is often accompanied by the rhythmic beating of a wooden drum. Traditionally, the dance was performed by males. However, females also participate in Siva Afi and often perform with ceremonial knives.

Siva

This is a graceful dance where women use gentle hand and arm movements to tell stories through dance.
